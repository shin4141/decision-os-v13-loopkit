# Field Notes as Low-Cost Future-Line Farming

## Observation

Field Notes can act like seed planting.

A conversation may not produce an immediate deliverable.

No paper may be written.

No README may be changed.

No product may be launched.

But the conversation can still produce:

- a weak signal
- a future market hint
- a metaphor
- a failure pattern
- a distinction
- a discomfort
- a partially formed idea
- a future article angle
- a future product clue
- a future research section

If captured cheaply, these fragments become future-line seeds.

## Core claim

Field Notes are a way to grow future lines without damaging the current line.

They allow the user to continue the present task while preserving possible future value.

```text
Field Notes are low-cost future-line options.
```

They are not a task list.

They are not all meant to be executed.

They are a field where weak signals can survive long enough to reconnect.

## Current line vs future line

The current line is what must be protected now:

- active repo work
- active external submission
- paid offer shaping
- outreach pilot
- review response
- urgent survival work

The future line is different:

- not ready now
- not urgent now
- not allowed to hijack the current line
- but potentially valuable later

Field Notes allow future lines to be planted without forcing immediate action.

## Farming metaphor

A useful metaphor:

```text
Seed:
a weak but potentially valuable idea

Sprout:
a signal that appears again or connects to another note

Crop:
a note cluster that is ready to become an article, loop, service, product, paper, or post

Parked:
valuable but not for the current season

Compost:
captured material that no longer needs to remain active, but may still inform future soil
```

This avoids the false choice between:

- execute every idea
- discard every non-current idea

## Why this matters

Without Field Notes, valuable ideas often disappear.

With unmanaged Field Notes, valuable ideas become clutter.

The goal is neither total capture nor total deletion.

The goal is selective preservation plus periodic convergence.

## Future-line farming cycle

A possible cycle:

```text
conversation
-> capture weak signal
-> label as Seed / Sprout / Crop / Parked / Compost
-> periodically review related notes
-> check whether signals converge
-> decide GO / HOLD / CAP / BLOCK
```

This connects Field Notes to V13 loop governance.

## Relation to Subthreshold Signal Integration

A single seed may not justify action.

It may be only 0.3 or 0.5.

But if multiple independent notes point to the same pain, audience, next action, and boundary, they may converge into an actionable signal.

This does not mean simple arithmetic.

False integration risk remains.

Recent signals should not be combined only because they are recent or emotionally salient.

The test is whether they point to the same future line.

## Market-entry implication

Field Notes can also preserve future market hints.

A strong future-line opportunity may appear when:

```text
internal Field Note convergence
x external market pain
x low-cost test path
```

This can make new-market entry less random.

Instead of chasing every trend, the user can ask:

- have we seen this pain internally before?
- do multiple notes point to the same unresolved need?
- is the external market starting to show the same pain?
- do we already have partial structure to solve it?
- can we test it without hijacking the current line?

## Risk: Field Note overgrowth

Field Notes can become valuable, but they can also become a forest.

Risks:

- too many notes to reconnect
- old notes treated as current
- weak ideas mistaken for commitments
- every seed treated as a task
- future lines hijacking the current line
- reconnectability debt

Therefore, Field Notes need status, not only accumulation.

## Suggested statuses

Use lightweight statuses when needed:

```text
Seed:
preserve only

Sprout:
watch for convergence

Crop:
ready for conversion

Parked:
valuable but not now

Compost:
do not actively use, but may inform future thinking
```

These are not mandatory labels for every note.

They are a review lens.

## Boundary

Do not create a new product now.

Do not create a new repo now.

Do not rewrite README now.

Do not add a Field Note registry now.

Do not convert every note into a task.

Do not use this as permission to expand the mainline.

This note records a future-line management principle.

## One-sentence summary

Field Notes let weak ideas become low-cost future-line seeds, but they must eventually be reviewed as Seed / Sprout / Crop / Parked / Compost so future value does not become reconnectability debt.
