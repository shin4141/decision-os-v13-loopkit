# Recap and Re-onboarding Cost

## Observation

A strong AI model still needs the recap.

In movies and serialized dramas, viewers can technically watch a later episode without the prior recap, but the experience is weaker. They may understand fragments, but they do not fully enter the story.

AI agents face a similar problem in long-running work.

If the next AI agent does not know:

- what happened last time
- what was proven
- what remains unverified
- what must not be touched
- why the previous loop stopped
- what the next safe action is

then the agent spends its intelligence reconstructing the past instead of advancing the work.

## Framing

Handoff is not paperwork.

Handoff is acceleration.

Without a recap, every new AI agent starts by rewatching the whole movie. Even a stronger model is forced to spend tokens, time, attention, and reasoning capacity on re-onboarding.

The cost is not only present cost. If handoff is skipped, the future pays the cost as:

- re-reading cost
- context reconstruction cost
- review burden
- accidental modification risk
- restart delay
- loss of route fidelity
- weaker returnability
