# Exposure Candidate Ledger

## Purpose

This ledger records possible exposure paths for V13 / LoopKit.

Exposure is not limited to social posting. It also includes places where a project can be submitted, listed, showcased, reviewed, or discovered later.

Examples:

- libraries
- directories
- newsletters
- showcases
- community examples
- tool lists
- grants
- hackathons

The purpose of this ledger is not to chase exposure immediately.

The purpose is to prevent useful exposure candidates from being lost, and to make future outreach decisions observable, capped, and reviewable.

## Core Rule

Exposure candidates may be recorded freely.

Submission does not happen automatically.

- Recording is GO.
- Exploration is CAP.
- Submission is HOLD or CAP.
- Mass outreach is BLOCK.

## Why This Exists

V13 / LoopKit discovered the Loop Library path by chance.

This ledger turns that accident into a reusable operating surface.

A useful exposure path should not depend on remembering it later from a long chat, social feed, or browser tab.

## Gate

| Action | Gate | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| Record a candidate | GO | Low-risk. Preserve the option. |
| Check fit | CAP | Limit to 1-2 candidates per week. |
| Prepare submission | HOLD / CAP | Requires fit, wording, and current capacity check. |
| Submit one candidate | CAP | One bounded submission at a time. |
| Mass submit / mass post | BLOCK | Too much exposure debt and credibility risk. |

## Status Definitions

- PARKED: candidate recorded, no action yet.
- CHECK: candidate is being evaluated for fit.
- SUBMIT: ready for bounded submission.
- DONE: submitted, listed, or otherwise completed.
- REJECT: not suitable, not worth pursuing, or rejected externally.

## Review Cadence

Default cadence:

- review at most 1-2 candidates per week
- do not open a broad search loop unless explicitly gated
- do not submit while tired, rushed, or unclear on pain translation

## Submission Preconditions

Before any submission, the candidate must pass:

1. Who is the audience?
2. What current pain does V13 / LoopKit reduce for them?
3. What is the lowest-friction next action?
4. What is the risk of looking spammy, too abstract, or premature?
5. Can this be submitted without creating new maintenance debt?

If these cannot be answered, keep the candidate PARKED or CHECK.
